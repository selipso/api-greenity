Know Your Environmental Health Risks

What Are You Breathing?

Manufacturing jobs can be a boom to local economies, but they can also spell shortened lifespans for all nearby residents. Although manufacturing today is much cleaner than it used to be, there are still unregulared chemicals being released into the atmosphere by some industries.

Even in 2017, more than half our energy comes from coal. Most of the rest comes from natural gas. Although natural gas is cleaner, both fuels still release Carbon Dioxide into our atmosphere, leading to global climate change and unpredictable, catastrophic natural disasters. And that's just point-source air pollution, or pollution from a single big polluter like a factory or manufacturing facility.

Distributed air pollution is more difficult to track, but can be just as dangerous in the long run. Pollution and greenhouse gases released in urban areas from large amounts of automobiles can create a thick smog layer for everyone in the area, making breathing every day as dangerous as smoking a pack of cigarettes.

Toxic Hazardous Waste

There are dangerous Superfund sites in every part of the country left over from the 60's and 70's. During this time, manufacturers were allowed to freely leech poisonous chemicals into our rivers, which soon contaminated our water supply.

They were also allowed to freely dump hazardous waste like battery acid, lead,and other dangerous materials into poorly built landfills. This hazardous waste made some areas so dangerous and uninhabitable that sometimes the trash would catch fire. When it rained, many of these chemicals would also make their way into our groundwater. These Superfund sites still exist today.
